About Me

I'm an undergraduate student currently in the third year of four-year B.Sc. degree in Computer Science with an Industrial Placement at Newcastle University. As part of my course I'm doing a year-long research placement in the group of Dr. Lucy Collinson at the Francis Crick Institute, working under the supervision of Dr. Martin Jones in the development of bioimage analysis plugins for napari that use machine learning techniques to automate the targeted analysis and segmentation of mitochondrial cristae in volume electron microscopy images. Currently interested in bioimage analysis and it's applications in Cancer research in the exploration of the tumour microenvironemt.

Favourite Recent Papers I've Read

1) SplineDist is an amazing instance segmentation CNN, built on the principles of StarDist whereby StarDist defines objects as star-convex polygons. SplineDist on the other hand employs a more flexible representation using splines, which can be defined as function defined piecewise by polynomials.

2) UTAG is a technique constructed for unsupervised discovery of tissue architecture with graphs. This technique was consistently able to detect higher-level architectures in human tissues and quantify structural differences between healthy and diseased tissues.

S. Mandal and V. Uhlmann, "Splinedist: Automated Cell Segmentation With Spline Curves," 2021 IEEE 18th International Symposium on Biomedical Imaging (ISBI), Nice, France, 2021, pp. 1082-1086, doi: 10.1109/ISBI48211.2021.9433928.

Kim, J., Rustam, S., Mosquera, J.M. et al. Unsupervised discovery of tissue architecture in multiplexed imaging. Nat Methods 19, 1653–1661 (2022). https://doi.org/10.1038/s41592-022-01657-2
